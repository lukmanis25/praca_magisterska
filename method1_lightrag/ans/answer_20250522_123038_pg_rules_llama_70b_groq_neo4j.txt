Question:
Under what circumstances can a student be removed from classes, and how is such an absence classified?

Answer:
**Removal from Classes**

According to the provided knowledge base, a student can be removed from classes in the following circumstances:

* **Unjustified absence from compulsory classes**: A student may be removed from the student register if they are absent from 4 classes as part of the same form of classes in the selected subject covered by the study plan.
* **Failure to submit a registration application**: If a student fails to submit a registration application, as provided for in ยง28(9).
* **Lack of progress in studies**: Students who have been registered and fail to complete the same semester three times may be excluded from the student register on account of lack of progress in their studies.

**Classification of Absence**

The absence of a student from classes can be classified as follows:

* **Sick leave**: A student may take a sick leave due to illness or injury.
* **Pregnant student leave**: A student may take a pregnant student leave during their pregnancy.
* **Parent leave**: A student may take a parent leave for childcare responsibilities.
* **Leave of absence**: A student can take one semester or one year off, except for paragraphs 2 and 3.

Please note that the specific classification of absence may depend on the circumstances and the decision made by the dean.
