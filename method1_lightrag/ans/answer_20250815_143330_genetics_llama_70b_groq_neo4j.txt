Answer:
**Griffith's Transformation Experiments**

In 1928, British scientist Frederick Griffith conducted a series of experiments that laid the foundation for the discovery of DNA as the genetic material. Griffith's experiments involved transforming non-pathogenic bacteria (R strain) into pathogenic bacteria (S strain) using a heat-killed S strain.

**The Experiment**

Griffith's experiment involved the following steps:

1.  He took two types of bacteria: the R strain, which was non-pathogenic, and the S strain, which was pathogenic.
2.  He killed the S strain by heat and then mixed it with the live R strain.
3.  He observed that the live R strain bacteria became pathogenic after being mixed with the heat-killed S strain.

**Conclusion**

Griffith concluded that the heat-killed S strain contained a transforming principle that could convert the non-pathogenic R strain into a pathogenic strain. He proposed that this transforming principle was responsible for the change in the bacteria's virulence.

**Implications**

Griffith's experiment had significant implications for the understanding of genetics and the discovery of DNA as the genetic material. His findings suggested that genetic information could be transferred from one cell to another, challenging the long-held belief that genetic information was fixed and immutable.
