Question:
What are the procedural requirements and limitations a student must meet when applying for reinstatement of student rights after removal from the student register?

Answer:
**Reinstatement of Student Rights Procedure**

To apply for reinstatement of student rights after removal from the student register, a student must meet the following procedural requirements and limitations:

**Eligibility**

* The student must have already studied at Gdańsk University of Technology and accumulated at least 45 ECTS credits at a given faculty.
* The student must not have been removed from the student register for more than 5 years.

**Application Process**

* The student must submit an application at the dean's office at least 30 days prior to the commencement of the semester in which the studies are to be resumed.
* The application must be made in person or through the "Moja PG" system.

**Conditions for Reinstatement**

* The student must fulfill the conditions set by the dean, which may include:
	+ Making up for differences in the curriculum within a scope and time limit set by the dean.
	+ Paying fees for repeated classes or subjects in accordance with the rector's regulation for the given academic year.
	+ Obtaining the dean's consent to resume studies in a related field, if the student was removed from the student register on courses that are no longer conducted at Gdańsk University of Technology.

**Limitations**

* A student who has been removed from the student register twice in the same field of study and cycle of studies may not apply for reinstatement.
* Reinstatement of student rights is not possible after 5 years from being removed from the student register.
