Question:
Under what circumstances can a student be removed from classes, and how is such an absence classified?

Answer:
**Removal from Classes: Circumstances and Classification**

According to the provided knowledge base, a student can be removed from classes in the following circumstances:

*   **Unjustified absence from 4 classes as part of the same form of classes in the selected subject covered by the study plan.** This implies that if a student fails to attend classes without a valid reason for 4 times within the same type of classes in a particular subject, they may be removed from the student register.

The absence is classified as **removal from the student register due to absence from compulsory classes.**

These circumstances are outlined in the provided text:

> 7. Removal from the student register due to absence from compulsory classes occurs in the event of unjustified absence from 4 classes as part of the same form of classes in the selected subject covered by the study plan.
